## 2. Core principles (the "why" layer)

All 19 principles read; the load-bearing ones:

1. **The above-the-fold decision happens in seconds** — the hero must communicate who it's for, the outcome, and the next step before any scroll (`the-above-the-fold-decision-happens-in-seconds`).
2. **Load speed is an upstream conversion filter** — the hero, demo, and proof only get a chance to work on visitors the page loaded fast enough to keep (`load-speed-is-an-upstream-conversion-filter-not-a-technical-afterthought`).
3. **Developers convert by trying, not by being sold to** — the highest-converting experience lets the visitor reach real value themselves: run a command, read working code, click a live demo (`developers-convert-by-trying-not-by-being-sold-to`).
4. **For dev tools the docs are the conversion funnel, not post-sale support** — a technical buyer decides by reaching a first success in the docs; every step to that success is a funnel stage (`for-dev-tools-the-docs-are-the-conversion-funnel-not-post-sale-support`).
5. **Every field and decision between intent and account is conversion lost** (`every-field-and-decision-between-intent-and-account-is-conversion-lost`).
6. **The header is conversion real estate, not a directory** — it is the only section visible from every scroll position (`the-header-is-the-only-section-visible-from-every-scroll-position-so-it-is-conversion-real-estate-not-a-directory`).
7. **Mobile is the first-impression and discovery surface even when conversion completes on desktop** (`mobile-is-the-first-impression-and-discovery-surface-even-when-conversion-completes-on-desktop`).
8. **Show the product working rather than describe what it does** (`show-the-product-working-rather-than-describe-what-it-does`).
9. **A pricing page converts by reducing decisions, not by listing options** (`a-pricing-page-converts-by-reducing-decisions-not-by-listing-options`).
10. **Open-core pricing splits by buyer persona, not by feature** — IC value stays open; management/org value (governance, SSO, compliance) is what converts (`open-core-pricing-splits-by-buyer-persona-not-by-feature`).
11. **Social proof must match the doubt and the buyer** — mismatched proof is noise at best, a credibility leak at worst (`social-proof-must-match-the-doubt-and-the-buyer-to-convert`).
12. **For a B2B dev tool the security review is a procurement gate** the page either pre-answers with self-serve proof or pays for as a multi-week delay: the average vendor security questionnaire runs 200+ questions across 19 risk domains and adds 2–6 weeks to an enterprise cycle (2–4 mid-market), almost entirely *after* the buyer has decided (`for-a-b2b-dev-tool-the-security-review-is-a-procurement-gate-the-page-either-pre-answers-or-pays-for-in-weeks`).
13. **An AI product page converts by calibrating trust, not maximizing the claim** (`an-ai-product-page-converts-by-calibrating-trust-not-by-maximizing-the-claim`); and **for an agentic product the conversion barrier is loss-of-control, not capability** — prove control and reversibility, not maximal autonomy (`for-an-agentic-product-the-conversion-barrier-is-loss-of-control-not-capability`).
14. **A comparison-page visitor has already decided to switch and only needs the verdict confirmed** — vs-pages are late-funnel permission, not category introduction (`a-comparison-page-visitor-has-already-decided-to-switch-and-only-needs-the-verdict-confirmed`).
15. **A page matched to one segment beats a page that serves everyone** (`a-page-matched-to-one-segment-beats-a-page-that-serves-everyone`).
16. **A pre-launch page sells a place in line, not the product** — minimize friction to the commitment, then immediately give the signup a reason to act again (`a-pre-launch-page-sells-a-place-in-line-not-the-product-itself`).
17. **For a dev tool the README is the first product experience and is judged in seconds** (`for-a-dev-tool-the-readme-is-the-first-product-experience-and-is-judged-in-seconds`).
18. **A compatibility section converts by removing the does-it-fit-my-stack objection**, not by decorating with logos (`a-compatibility-section-converts-by-removing-the-does-it-fit-my-stack-objection`).

## 3. Cluster-by-cluster: practices, pitfalls, and the mem's concrete specifics

### Cluster 38 — Mobile, header, CTA & sign-up

**Practices**
- **Mobile-first design; primary CTA in the thumb zone; every tap target ≥48×48 CSS px.** The thumb zone is roughly the bottom 40% of the screen (`mobile-conversion-surface-and-the-thumb-zone`). Recorded data: a checkout button pair mis-tapped ~11% of the time at 4pt separation dropped to ~2% at 12pt; CTAs ≥48×48px with ≥16px whitespace saw a 49% higher tap-through rate; spacing rules ≥8pt between ≥44pt targets, ≥16pt near screen edges to dodge OS gestures (`place-the-primary-mobile-cta-in-the-thumb-zone-and-size-every-tap-target-at-least-48px`, citing `72technologies-tap-targets-and-thumb-zones-beyond-the-44px-rule-2026`). Traffic backdrop: mobile is ~64% of global web traffic in 2026; ~79–83% of SaaS landing-page *visits* are mobile (Unbounce data), with SaaS mobile (~6.4%) and desktop (~6.2%) conversion near parity (`sq-magazine-and-unbounce-mobile-vs-desktop-traffic-and-saas-conversion-parity-2026`).
- **Header: ~5 links, logo top-left, single contrasting CTA top-right, quieter Login** (`hold-the-marketing-site-header-to-about-five-links-and-put-a-single-contrasting-cta-top-right`, citing HubSpot's 3–4-item pattern via `nerdcow-b2b-saas-website-navigation-tricks-2026` and `dorik-website-header-design-best-practices-2026`). For dev tools add **Docs and GitHub with a live star count** in the bar — persistent social proof at every scroll position; omit the count when small (`put-docs-and-github-with-a-live-star-count-in-the-developer-tool-header`). On a dedicated single-offer landing page, strip nav to logo + CTA (`strip-the-header-to-a-logo-and-single-cta-on-a-dedicated-landing-page`, condition `when-the-page-is-a-dedicated-single-offer-landing-page-not-the-home-or-product-page`).
- **Sign-up: ≤3 fields, defer the rest to onboarding.** Benchmarks: 15.2% conversion at 1–2 fields, 8.7% at 3–4, 4.3% at 5–6, 2.1% at 7+; Slack's 6→3-field cut lifted conversion 32%; a documented 11→4-field cut lifted 120%; Mailchimp's deferred-data approach lifted activation 43% (`cut-the-sign-up-form-to-three-fields-or-fewer-and-defer-the-rest-to-onboarding`). Offer SSO with **GitHub first** for dev tools (`offer-sso-sign-in-and-put-github-first-for-developer-tools`).
- **No-credit-card opt-in trial by default** (confidence: *debated*): opt-out (card-required) trials convert 49–60% trial-to-paid but only ~28.4% retain at 6 months; opt-in converts 18–25% but retains better and pulls 8.5% visitor-to-signup vs ~2.5% card-gated (`default-to-a-no-credit-card-opt-in-trial-to-maximize-qualified-sign-ups`, citing `amra-and-elma-free-trial-conversion-statistics-2026`; bounded by `when-the-motion-is-sales-led-or-high-acv`).
- **Frame the CTA as a bounded trial**: "Trial for free" vs "Sign up for free" produced a 104% MoM increase in trial starts in the Going / Scott's Cheap Flights case (`frame-the-cta-as-a-bounded-trial-not-an-open-ended-sign-up`, `gemx-ab-test-homepage-cta-trial-for-free-2025`). Pin risk-reducer microcopy ("No credit card required") under the CTA and next to the header CTA (`pin-risk-reducer-microcopy-directly-under-the-cta-button`, `pin-objection-answering-microcopy-next-to-the-header-cta`). Repeat the primary CTA down-page and sticky (`repeat-the-primary-cta-down-the-page-and-make-it-sticky-on-scroll`).

**Pitfalls**: desktop-first squeeze-down (`shipping-a-desktop-first-landing-page-that-only-gets-squeezed-down-for-mobile`); horizontal-scroll tables on mobile (`forcing-a-wide-comparison-table-or-feature-grid-to-horizontal-scroll-on-mobile`); tap targets under ~44–48px (`tap-targets-too-small-or-too-crowded-to-hit-reliably-on-a-phone`); Login styled like the primary CTA (`giving-the-header-login-the-same-visual-weight-as-the-primary-cta`); sitemap-header (`filling-the-converting-page-header-with-a-full-sitemap-of-links`); card-gating a self-serve trial (`requiring-a-credit-card-on-a-self-serve-product-to-start-a-trial`); asking role/company/use-case pre-account (`asking-for-company-role-and-use-case-before-the-account-exists`).

**Applicability**: strictly sales-led B2B desktop-research products invert the mobile priority — one cut reports B2B SaaS at ~65% desktop / ~35% mobile (`when-the-product-is-strictly-b2b-sales-led-and-buyers-research-on-desktop`).

### Cluster 39 — Page body, demo/docs-led conversion, pricing, comparison

**Practices**
- **Body order mirrors the developer evaluation flow**: problem → solution → What's-Inside → demo/code → proof → pricing → FAQ → repeated CTA (`order-the-devtool-page-body-as-problem-solution-proof-demo-pricing-faq`, citing `whoffagents-devtool-landing-page-patterns-that-work-2025`).
- **Real syntax-highlighted code within the first ~1,000px of scroll**; daily.dev's "Time to Code" rule says pushing first code beyond ~1,000px risks losing up to 50% of technical traffic. Lead with the "magic line", semantic values not foo/bar, real highlighter (shiki/prism) with copy button, language-toggle tabs (`put-real-syntax-highlighted-code-within-the-first-scroll`; pitfall `showing-fake-or-placeholder-code-in-the-demo-section`; caveat `when-the-buyer-is-non-technical-or-the-product-is-no-code`).
- **Ungated interactive demo**: Navattic's 2026 benchmark across ~5,000 B2B SaaS sites — 66% of top-performing demos are ungated; ungated sees ~6% higher engagement, ~7% higher completion; 86% built from real HTML/CSS captures; best flows 5–13 steps (1–6 highest completion), opening modal in 71.9% of top demos, ~25–30 words per dialog, ~4–5 CTAs, 62% placed on product/solution pages (`embed-an-ungated-interactive-demo-on-the-product-page-not-a-book-a-demo-gate`, citing `navattic-interactive-demo-best-practices-and-2026-benchmarks`).
- **Docs-led conversion**: quickstart engineered for time-to-first-call under 5 minutes (Stripe-grade: 2–3 min; >30 min is a serious DX problem; onboarding friction abandons 20–40% of users; target 40–60% of signups making a first call within 7 days) (`engineer-the-quickstart-for-time-to-first-call-under-five-minutes`). Instrument docs as a funnel with micro-conversions — snippet copies, key generation, first 2xx; 401/403 spikes = unclear auth docs, 400/422 = incomplete schema examples, 429 = trial quota too tight (`instrument-the-docs-as-a-measurable-funnel-with-micro-conversions`, citing `fern-developer-documentation-metrics-that-matter-2026`). Ungated docs playground returning a real response with no signup (`offer-an-ungated-docs-playground-that-returns-a-real-response-with-no-signup`).
- **Features: 3–6 core blocks, outcome-headed, real product UI** — past ~8 blocks the section dilutes (`show-three-to-six-core-features-and-cut-the-rest`, `write-feature-block-headings-as-outcomes-not-feature-names`, `lead-every-feature-block-with-real-product-ui-not-an-abstract-illustration`). Layout follows feature type: alternating rows / bento / belts / persona tabs / steps (`pick-the-feature-section-layout-from-the-feature-type`).
- **Pricing: exactly three self-serve tiers, enterprise separated out** — one team cutting 5→3 tiers (plus a Most Popular badge) lifted conversion 1.2%→3.1% (`offer-exactly-three-self-serve-tiers-and-separate-enterprise-out`); center "Most Popular" badge (`highlight-one-recommended-tier-with-a-most-popular-badge-in-the-center`); transparency matched to ACV — full prices under ~$25K, "from $X" for ~$25K–100K, contact-sales only for true enterprise (`match-pricing-transparency-to-deal-size-and-show-a-starting-price-on-the-enterprise-tier`); annual default framed as absolute dollars/free months (`default-to-annual-billing-and-frame-the-discount-as-absolute-dollars-saved`); free-tier limits in buyer-language units before signup (`state-the-free-tier-usage-limit-in-buyer-language-units-before-signup`).
- **Open-core pricing**: unlimited self-host open tier; paid line at org-level value (SSO, approvals, audit); 74% of organizations using OSS will pay for improved security/maintenance/compliance; hosted free tier capped to create the upgrade trigger while the self-host download carries no imposed limits (`frame-the-open-source-tier-as-an-unlimited-self-host-option-and-draw-the-paid-line-at-org-level-value`, citing `open-core-ventures-a-standard-pricing-model-for-open-core-2023` — OCV founded by GitLab co-founder Sid Sijbrandij — and `monetizely-monetizing-open-source-pricing-strategies-for-open-core-saas-2026`). Self-host vs cloud is a price/operational-burden choice, not a feature gate (`present-self-host-versus-cloud-as-a-price-and-operational-burden-choice-not-a-feature-gate`).
- **Comparison pages**: open with the verdict and 1–3 differentiators (`open-a-vs-competitor-page-with-the-verdict-and-one-to-three-differentiators`); build the table from specific values ("p99 latency 40ms", "$269/mo") with a why-it-matters column, 8–12 visible rows, concede 1–2 rows to the competitor, use-case-conditional verdict (`build-the-comparison-table-from-specific-metrics-and-admit-your-own-limitations`); pair with a phased migration guide and switching incentive (`pair-the-comparison-page-with-a-phased-migration-guide-and-a-switching-incentive`); time pushes to a competitor price hike, outage, or deprecation (`time-comparison-page-pushes-to-a-competitor-price-hike-outage-or-deprecation`).
- **Integrations**: recognizable-logo belt plus real connecting code behind language-toggle tabs (`build-the-integrations-belt-from-logos-the-buyer-recognizes-and-depends-on`, `prove-ecosystem-fit-with-code-and-language-toggle-tabs-not-just-a-logo-wall`). For MCP-native AI products: "one integration works in Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and any MCP client" — MCP hit 97M+ installs as of March 2026 (`frame-ai-product-compatibility-as-works-with-every-mcp-client-from-one-integration`, citing `ddr-innova-mcp-97-million-installs-ai-integration-standard-2026`).
- **FAQ**: 10–20 customer-worded questions, grouped by objection category (Getting Started / Compatibility / Pricing & Limits / Security / Self-host), answer-first sentences, FAQPage JSON-LD; above ~20 add search (`structure-the-faq-as-a-customer-worded-accordion-grouped-by-objection-category-with-faqpage-schema`); conversion-critical objections belong on-page, not in the FAQ (`when-the-objection-is-conversion-critical-it-belongs-on-page-not-in-the-faq`).
- **Team invites as the paid-conversion predictor** for the post-first-success path (`build-the-page-cta-around-team-invites-as-the-paid-conversion-predictor`); product-specific CTA copy over generic "Get started" (`write-product-specific-cta-copy-not-generic-get-started`, per `evil-martians-100-devtool-landing-pages-study-2025`).

**Pitfalls**: hiding all prices behind Contact-sales on a self-serve product (`hiding-all-prices-behind-contact-sales-on-a-self-serve-product`); 5+ tiers / feature-stuffed cards (`offering-five-or-more-tiers-or-stuffing-every-feature-into-each-card`); clawing back previously-open features (`clawing-back-previously-open-features-behind-the-paywall`); checkmark-soup and deck-stacking comparison tables (`filling-the-comparison-table-with-checkmarks-instead-of-data`, `stacking-the-comparison-deck-so-you-win-every-row`); gated demos and gated quickstarts (`gating-the-interactive-demo-behind-an-email-form`, `gating-the-quickstart-behind-signup-so-first-success-comes-after-the-account`); FAQ softballs (`padding-the-faq-with-marketing-softballs-instead-of-real-objections`); aspirational/low-contrast integration logos (`padding-the-integrations-belt-with-aspirational-or-unrecognized-logos`, `low-contrast-integration-logos-that-disappear-against-the-section-background`); abstract 3D feature decoration (`decorating-the-features-section-with-abstract-3d-graphics-instead-of-the-product`).

### Cluster 42 — README as landing page

**Practices**: first ~600px judged like a hero; above-the-fold order converging across four 2025–2026 teardowns: (1) H1 + logo, (2) one-liner ≤10 words, (3) demo GIF/screenshot — the single highest-leverage element (GIFs 15–30s, under 5MB per Iris Wei), (4) 3–5 functional badges, (5) quickstart of ≤3 copy-paste commands (~30s to working state); whole file ~800–1,500 words (`order-the-readme-above-the-fold-as-one-liner-demo-badges-then-quickstart`, `open-the-readme-with-a-concrete-one-liner-of-ten-words-or-fewer`, `show-the-tool-working-with-a-demo-gif-or-screenshot-in-the-first-readme-viewport`, `put-a-copy-paste-quickstart-of-three-steps-or-fewer-near-the-top-of-the-readme`; skeleton in `above-the-fold-readme-skeleton-for-a-developer-cli-tool`). Badges: license, CI status, package version, last-commit/stars; skip download-count and coverage-% as vanity; sources differ on ceiling (3–5 per Iris Wei/Belal Zahran vs up to 7 per River) but agree 8+ is clutter (`limit-readme-badges-to-three-to-five-functional-trust-signals`). ### Cluster 43 — Hero, Core Web Vitals, AI-product trust

**Practices**
- **Hero**: outcome-led headline graspable in ~5 seconds (`lead-the-hero-headline-with-the-outcome-not-the-feature`); when a defensible quantified claim exists, make the number the H1 — in DigitalApplied's 2,000-page A/B meta-study (Q4 2025–Q1 2026, 95% significance, 1,000+ sessions/variant) the single-stat headline was the top hero pattern at +18% vs image-hero control, beating customer-quote (+12%) and product-screenshot (+9%); autoplay video scored −7% and stock photography −11% (`lead-the-hero-with-one-quantified-claim-as-the-headline`, `digitalapplied-landing-page-conversion-study-2000-pages-2026`). Quantified named-customer proof next to the hero claim ("Used by 8 of the Fortune 50") — named-customer count with quantified scope was +22% vs single testimonial +14% / logo strip +8% (`place-a-quantified-named-customer-proof-point-next-to-the-hero-claim`). Dual hero CTA for dev tools: bold product action + lighter docs/source path (`use-a-dual-hero-cta-pairing-a-product-action-with-a-docs-or-source-path`, per Evil Martians). Same study: sticky-bottom CTA +11%, above-fold +6%, both +12% — no compounding.
- **Performance**: sub-2s LCP as a hard conversion budget (2.5s "Good" ceiling) — bounce climbs 26% (0–1s) → 38% (2–3s) → 52% (3–5s); each extra second costs ~7% of conversions (~1% per 100ms); sub-2s sites convert ~23% higher than over-4s; only 42% of mobile sites pass all three CWV (2026: LCP 58%/43% desktop/mobile, INP 78%/65%, CLS 78%/74%) (`treat-sub-2-second-lcp-as-a-conversion-budget-for-the-landing-page-hero`, `digitalapplied-page-speed-statistics-2026-revenue-impact`). Defer non-critical JS to keep INP under 200ms (`defer-non-critical-javascript-so-the-hero-cta-is-interactive-fast`); reserve media/font space to keep CLS under 0.1 (`reserve-space-for-hero-media-and-fonts-to-keep-cls-in-the-good-range`); measure field data on mid-range mobile, not office wifi (`measuring-landing-page-speed-only-on-a-fast-desktop-on-office-wifi`).
- **AI-product trust**: state the control boundary in the hero ("AI drafts, you approve") (`state-the-ai-control-boundary-in-the-hero-for-ai-products`, concept `ai-control-boundary-messaging`); lead agentic pages with *scoped* autonomy — buyer confidence in fully autonomous agents fell 43%→22% in a year as production failures surfaced (`lead-an-agentic-product-page-with-scoped-autonomy-not-full-autonomy`, citing `generative-inc-agentic-ai-2026-from-chatting-to-doing`; converting 2026 position is human-in-the-loop per `autonomy-control-spectrum-for-agentic-products`); carry the boundary into the body, show the audit trail and rollback as a first-class section (`show-the-human-in-the-loop-boundary-in-the-body-not-just-the-hero-for-agentic-products`, `show-the-audit-trail-and-reversibility-as-a-first-class-section-for-agentic-products`); show a clean human handoff on failure (`show-the-clean-human-handoff-on-agent-failure-as-a-trust-signal`); prove agency with an end-to-end task run, not a chat transcript (`prove-the-agent-actually-acts-by-showing-an-end-to-end-task-run-not-a-chat-transcript`).
- **Accuracy and benchmarks**: state a measured accuracy figure plus the grounding method; frontier hallucination rates run roughly 4–19% in 2026, so credible accuracy claims sit in the 80s–90s, never 100% (`state-a-measured-accuracy-figure-and-the-grounding-method-instead-of-claiming-the-ai-is-never-wrong`, citing `digitalapplied-ai-model-hallucination-rate-benchmarks-2026-study`, `nng-ai-hallucinations-what-designers-need-to-know-2025`). Use task-grounded benchmarks (SWE-Bench, tool-call success, retrieval faithfulness, eval-on-your-data) — every frontier model now exceeds ~88% on MMLU so generic leaderboard scores prove nothing; prefer contamination-resistant suites (LiveCodeBench harvests post-cutoff problems; GDPval uses blind expert grading) (`prove-an-ai-product-with-task-grounded-benchmarks-not-saturated-leaderboard-scores`, citing `kili-technology-ai-benchmarks-guide-top-evaluations-2026-and-why-they-are-not-enough`). Make every benchmark auditable: link scores to test cases, version-pin, state methodology, report multi-run consistency (`make-every-benchmark-claim-auditable-by-linking-the-score-to-its-test-cases-and-pinning-the-version`, `report-multi-run-consistency-or-your-data-eval-not-a-single-best-run-benchmark-number`). - **AI-written copy**: edit out tonality tells — the 2,000-page study measured >2 em-dashes/100 words at −5%, "leverage"/"optimize your experience" at −8%, generic adjectives −4% (`shipping-unedited-ai-generated-hero-copy-with-detectable-tonality-tells`, `digitalapplied-landing-page-conversion-study-2000-pages-2026`).

## 5. Three defensible, non-obvious recommendations

**1. If the page already has a sticky-bottom CTA, do not add a second above-the-fold CTA expecting compound lift — and prefer sticky-bottom if you pick one.** DigitalApplied's 2,000-page meta-study measured sticky-bottom CTA at +11%, above-fold at +6%, and both together at +12% — effectively no compounding (`digitalapplied-landing-page-conversion-study-2000-pages-2026`; caveat `when-a-sticky-cta-is-already-present`; practices `repeat-the-primary-cta-down-the-page-and-make-it-sticky-on-scroll`, `place-the-primary-mobile-cta-in-the-thumb-zone-and-size-every-tap-target-at-least-48px`, which notes the sticky bar "carries almost all the addressable lift"). In review: fight the instinct to stack CTAs; spend the effort on the sticky bar's thumb-zone ergonomics instead.

**2. In the vs-competitor table, deliberately concede one or two rows to the competitor and admit a limitation of your own.** The comparison-page visitor is late-funnel, has effectively decided to switch, and is fact-checking for permission (`a-comparison-page-visitor-has-already-decided-to-switch-and-only-needs-the-verdict-confirmed`); a table you win 100% of reads as biased and one detected exaggeration costs the whole page its credibility (`stacking-the-comparison-deck-so-you-win-every-row`). Build rows from specific values with a why-it-matters column, cap at 8–12 visible rows, and frame the verdict use-case-conditionally (`build-the-comparison-table-from-specific-metrics-and-admit-your-own-limitations`). Counterintuitive in a design review — marketing will resist green checks in the competitor's column — but the mem's evidence says the concession is what makes your winning rows believable.

**3. For a self-serve dev tool, choose the no-credit-card opt-in trial and defend the *lower* trial-to-paid number.** Card-gated trials show 49–60% trial-to-paid vs 18–25% for opt-in — the naive dashboard argument favors the card. But only ~28.4% of card-gated conversions retain at 6 months (near half churn before second renewal), and the card wall crushes top-of-funnel: 8.5% visitor-to-signup opt-in vs ~2.5% card-gated (`default-to-a-no-credit-card-opt-in-trial-to-maximize-qualified-sign-ups`, citing `amra-and-elma-free-trial-conversion-statistics-2026`). Pair it with bounded-trial CTA framing ("Trial for free", +104% trial starts in the Going case, `gemx-ab-test-homepage-cta-trial-for-free-2025`) and risk-reducer microcopy under the button (`pin-risk-reducer-microcopy-directly-under-the-cta-button`). The mem marks this `debated` and scopes it: it inverts for sales-led/high-ACV motions (`when-the-motion-is-sales-led-or-high-acv`) — which is exactly the applicability argument to bring to the review.

(Runner-up: move the SOC 2 badge from the footer to beside the pricing CTA as a verifiable AICPA-linked artifact — reported 10–20% lift and it pre-answers a 200-question, 2–6-week procurement gate; `place-the-soc-2-badge-at-the-decision-moment-with-a-verifiable-link-not-buried-in-the-footer`, `for-a-b2b-dev-tool-the-security-review-is-a-procurement-gate-the-page-either-pre-answers-or-pays-for-in-weeks`.)
